Medicines come in a variety of different formulations depending on how they are to be taken or used.

Pharmaceutical formulation is the process of combining various chemical substances with the active drug to form a final medicinal product, which is called a drug mixture or drug formulation.

A drug formulation can be given to the patient in various forms like solid, semisolid or liquid. The type of the formulation given depends upon the patientâ€™s age, sex, and health condition and is specific for particular routes of administration. Here we give a brief overview of the different forms of drug formulation development:

Tablets

a tablet is disc-shaped and prepared by compressing a granulated powder in a die of suitable machinery. They are mostly coated with inert substances like starch to help them disintegrate in the digestive tract of the patient. A binding agent, lubricating material, and flavors are added to the tablets to make them palatable.

Controlled Release Tablet

is designed to release the active ingredient of the drug in a specific amount over a specified period of time.
